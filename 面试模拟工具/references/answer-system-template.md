# Answer System Template

## Difficulty Selection Checklist
A good difficulty:
- Requires a decision or prioritization.
- Involves real trade-offs and alternatives.
- Impacts user experience, system design, or product outcomes.
- Demonstrates PM thinking (problem framing, constraints, impact).

If the candidate difficulty is only “a hard bug,” reframe to a decision with product impact.

## Evidence Map Template
Use a compact map to keep claims honest.

Format:
- Claim: <statement>
  - Evidence: <file/section/source> OR Assumption: <explicit label>

## Three-Layer Output Template

### Difficulty Selected
<1–2 lines describing the decision-value difficulty>

### Evidence Map

### Layer 1 — Surface Answer (30–60 sec)
- Situation
- Conflict / Decision point
- Action / Choice
- Result / Impact
- Lesson (transferable capability)

### Layer 2 — Deep Breakdown (not necessarily spoken)
- Constraints
- Alternatives considered
- Trade-offs and rationale
- Failed attempt or uncertainty
- What you would do differently next time

### Layer 3 — Follow-up Defense
Generate 3–7 likely follow-ups:
- Why not alternative X?
- How did you know this was the biggest difficulty?
- What was the measurable or qualitative impact?
- What would you do if you had more time/resources?
- What did you learn that transfers to a new domain?

## Compression Levels
- 30 sec version
- 2 min version
- Deep dive (expand trade-offs, evidence, and outcomes)

## Language Guidance
- Prefer decision framing over implementation details.
- Avoid invented metrics; use qualitative signals if needed.
- Use concrete nouns and verbs; avoid generic buzzwords.
