# Scoring Rubrics

Use consistent scales. If you choose 1-5 or 1-10, keep it stable across all items.

## Common Criteria (1-5 scale)

User Value:
- 1: Minor convenience, low frequency
- 3: Clear improvement to a common task
- 5: Breaks a major bottleneck or repeated pain

Business Impact:
- 1: Minimal impact on core metrics
- 3: Moderate impact on one key metric
- 5: Strong impact on multiple key metrics

Strategic Alignment:
- 1: Tangential to current strategy
- 3: Supports one strategic pillar
- 5: Directly advances the top strategic goal

Effort (reverse scale preferred in weighted model):
- 1: Multi-team, multi-quarter
- 3: Single team, several sprints
- 5: Small scope, 1-2 sprints

Risk (penalty):
- 1: Low risk, reversible
- 3: Moderate risk, some unknowns
- 5: High risk, hard to reverse or high trust cost

## RICE Inputs

Reach:
- Define unit (users per month, teams per quarter) and use that consistently.

Impact:
- 0.25 minimal, 0.5 low, 1.0 medium, 2.0 high, 3.0 massive

Confidence:
- 0.2 low, 0.5 medium, 0.8 high

Effort:
- Person-months or person-weeks, be consistent.

## ICE Inputs

Impact: 1-10
Confidence: 1-10
Effort: 1-10 (lower is better)

ICE Score = (Impact * Confidence) / Effort

## MoSCoW

Must: cannot ship without it
Should: high value, but not critical
Could: nice to have
Won't: not now

Use MoSCoW as a category label, not a numeric score.
