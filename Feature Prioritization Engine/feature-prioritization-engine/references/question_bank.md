# Question Bank - Feature Prioritization Engine

Use small batches (5-8 questions). Prefer questions that change scoring or invalidate assumptions.

## Stage 1

1. Who is the primary user segment and how many users are in it?
2. What specific problem does this feature solve, and how is it observed today?
3. What is the expected user value (time saved, errors reduced, delight)?
4. What business goal does it support (growth, retention, revenue, cost)?
5. What is the success signal we would measure?
6. What are the main dependencies or constraints?

## Stage 2

1. What is the current strategy focus (growth, retention, profitability, trust)?
2. Which criteria matter most this quarter, and why?
3. Do we need a framework that favors speed (ICE) or evidence (RICE)?
4. Are there non-negotiables (compliance, platform parity, infra stability)?

## Stage 3

1. What is the basis for Reach (user count, segment size, frequency)?
2. What evidence supports Impact (qualitative or quantitative)?
3. How strong is Confidence (data quality, sample size, recency)?
4. What is the Effort range (design, eng, QA, rollout)?
5. What risk could invalidate the expected impact?

## Stage 4

1. Which items are quick wins versus strategic bets?
2. What are the top 3 trade-offs if we pick the top scored items?
3. Which item unlocks or blocks others (dependencies)?

## Stage 5

1. Is the portfolio balanced across short-term wins and long-term bets?
2. Are we over-invested in any one segment or channel?
3. What must be done to reduce risk in the top 2 items?

## Stage 6

1. What is the top-ranked item and the reasoning in one sentence?
2. What is the single biggest uncertainty in this ranking?
3. What is the smallest experiment to reduce that uncertainty?
